## Tromsø (2024/1/30–2/1)

### Before you go
- Getting there: it's at 69°N, inside the Arctic Circle. You can take a night bus from Rovaniemi or fly (similar prices). Finnair with baggage was about NT$4,100, connecting in Helsinki
- Around town: buy tickets in the Troms Billett app — a single is about 40 NOK (NOK to TWD is roughly 1:3), 24-hour ticket 120 NOK, seven-day ticket 280 NOK (if you're staying more than two days, just get the seven-day). The same tickets cover the airport run, and you don't tap on or off
- Tours: northern lights and whale watching (whale season runs from mid-October to the end of January) are each about NT$3,000–5,000. In good weather you can see the aurora from town or the cable car anyway
- Worth seeing: the Arctic Cathedral (70 NOK), the Polar Museum, Tromsø University Museum (free for students), the cable car (415 NOK return)

### What we actually did
1/31 Tromsø University Museum → the Arctic Cathedral → cable car → the library
(Our HEL–TOS flight was cancelled because of the weather and we lost a day, so we missed both the aurora and whale tours. Do take the cable car — the view from the top is gorgeous.)

### Food
We saved money by buying and cooking from supermarkets. Norwegian chocolate milk is delicious (the strawberry one too). Pasta from SPAR 120 NOK, instant noodles and salad from KIWI 84.5 NOK

### Where we stayed
An Airbnb near the centre (18 minutes by bus to the airport), about NT$1,800 per person per night for three nights. Careful on the slopes when it snows

### What I learned
From the cancelled flight: book on the airline's own site and they'll rebook you and cover accommodation automatically. If you're travelling as a group, book together — separate bookings get split up. This was a big step for me as a solo traveller: I struck up a conversation with another Taiwanese person while boarding and we did the cable car together (I nearly slid into a gully without crampons). Don't get too gloomy when things go wrong — it all works out ✨

### What it cost
- Accommodation: NT$5,400
- Transport: NT$360 (+1,245 for the cable car)
- Food: NT$660
- Souvenirs: about NT$200

## Bergen (2024/2/1)

- Getting there: TOS→BGO with baggage was about NT$3,900. From the airport, the light rail is 44 NOK and valid for 90 minutes (the ride takes about 40); the ticket stays in the app rather than printing
- What we did: Torget fish market → the Bryggen wharf → Asian Street Food (Nordic food is expensive and unremarkable, so we ate Asian; beef ball noodle soup 120 NOK)
- Never got round to the famous fish soup at Søstrene Hagelin
- Verdict: honestly a fairly dull city — don't give it too much time. The weather changes incredibly fast, from snow to sunshine in 20 minutes
- What it cost: food NT$360, souvenirs NT$230

## Norway in a Nutshell (2024/2/1–2/2)

- Two days, one night: Bergen 15:41 train → Myrdal → the Flåm Railway → Flåm (overnight) → fjord cruise → Gudvangen → (should have been bus → Voss → train → Oslo, but an avalanche warning turned it into a six-hour coach)
- Book transport in the VY app (student fares available); book the cruise on the Norway's Best site. A Eurail Pass covers the trains (second class needs a reservation) and gets 30% off the Flåm Railway
- 📍 Ægir Bryggeri Pub (Flåm): the best meal I had in Europe! The pork ribs are wonderful (Asian-style chilli, not at all heavy) and so are the roast potatoes, about NT$850 a head
- Where we stayed: Brekke Apartments (via Booking), NT$1,548 per person per night, 20 minutes' walk from the centre, and they'll pick you up if you book before 8pm (150 NOK per car)
- Verdict: stunning, you have to do it 💕 The Flåm Railway is the best part — do it in daylight. In good weather the cruise gives you the fjord in full
- What it cost: accommodation 1,548, transport about 5,000 (the Eurail Pass saved about 1,200), food about 2,800

## Oslo (2024/2/2–2/3)

- Getting around: the airport express (fast, expensive, frequent, you must tap your ticket) or the ordinary train (covered by Eurail, 20–30 minutes into the city). The flight back to London Stansted was NT$590 (baggage included with the ESN card)
- Wanted to go and didn't: the National Museum, for Munch's The Scream
- Where I stayed: Comfort Hotel Xpress Central Station, NT$1,658 a night, 5 minutes from the station, clean but no kettle
- Verdict: a Nordic city that really feels like a capital. I'd like to come back and do it properly
- What it cost: accommodation NT$1,568
