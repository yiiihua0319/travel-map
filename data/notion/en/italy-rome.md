# 🇮🇹 Rome and Naples (with Vatican City)

## Rome (2023/11/30–12/4, my second visit)

### Getting around
- Two airports: FCO (convenient for the centre) and Ciampino (shuttle bus €12 return, about 40 minutes)
- In the city: a day metro ticket is €7, tap your card on the bus. The sights are far apart on foot — I did nearly 20,000 steps a day

### What to see
- ⚠️ If you want to visit the underground level of the Colosseum, **book on the official site a month ahead** (you get photos of the arena with nobody in it). Book the Vatican Museums in advance too and skip the queue
- Worth seeing: the Colosseum, Roman Forum, Trevi Fountain, Temple of Hadrian, Pantheon, Castel Sant'Angelo, Vatican Museums (student tickets available), St. Peter's Basilica (**go in the evening** — no queue, and it's staggering), Spanish Steps, Baths of Caracalla, Pincian Hill (for sunset)
- 💫 12/1 Colosseum → Roman Forum → Pantheon → Temple of Hadrian → Pincian Hill → St. Peter's Basilica; 12/2 Vatican Museums → Spanish Steps → Trevi Fountain → Castel Sant'Angelo

### Food
- Desserts: 📍 Pompi (the famous tiramisu, there's a branch at the station) 📍 Giolitti (famous gelato — pistachio and chocolate) 📍 Guttilla Alta Gelateria (the berry is excellent, plus pistachio cream) 📍 L'Arena Del Gelato (lemon, to cut the richness)
- Meals: 📍 Iari The Vino (unbelievably good seafood pasta! near the Colosseum, the pizza is great too) 📍 Ristorante Pizzeria Castello (tomato and seafood risotto, prawn pizza) 📍 Borghiciana Pastificio Artigianale (order the lasagne)

### Where I stayed
An Airbnb near Cipro, about NT$1,412 per person per night

### What I thought
Be prepared to walk yourself into the ground. The Vatican is a must — it's home to the only Taiwanese embassy you'll see there; the organ in St. Peter's brought me to tears; and the Vatican Museums are worth it just for the Sistine Chapel ceiling (no photos allowed).

### What it cost
Accommodation about NT$5,000, transport about NT$1,000, food about NT$4,000

## Naples (day trip on 2023/12/3)

- Getting there: train from Rome. Buying a return on the day cost €30 (not worth it — book earlier)
- What we did: Castel Sant'Elmo
- 📍 Antica Pizzeria Port'Alba: a pizzeria dating from 1738, about €18 a head, chewy base, every flavour good (the one highlight of the day)
- ⚠️ Watch your things: a group of pickpockets on the metro nearly got into my friend's pocket. The further south you go in Italy, the more careful you need to be
- Verdict: fairly dull in winter — I'd go to the nearby islands or Pompeii instead
