# 🇮🇹 Pisa, Cinque Terre and Florence

## Pisa (2024/1/19–1/23, our base)

- Getting there: a direct return from Manchester to Pisa was NT$1,000 without baggage! The airport train into town is €13 return; the centre is walkable
- What to do (half a day is plenty): the Leaning Tower and Pisa Cathedral (the cathedral is free on Sundays)
- Food: 📍 Pamino (focaccia sandwiches, the local speciality, €7.5 and enormous — get the pistachio cream) 📍 Misù Gourmet (tiramisu €6)
- Where we stayed: Rooms in Chiassetto (via Agoda), €59 for three nights = NT$668 a night! Shared bathroom, kettle in the room, 10 minutes from the station
- Verdict: a small country town where the tower is the only real draw. There's one good shopping street (H&M had boots down from €50 to €15)
- What it cost: accommodation 2,006, airport transport 442, food about 900, souvenirs 70

## Cinque Terre (day trip on 2024/1/20)

- Getting there: Pisa → La Spezia is an hour by train (under €10 each way). Between the villages the only option is the train: off-season €3 single or €15 day pass (in peak season €5 / €18). The day pass also covers the free shuttle bus up the hill in three of the villages. Trains run every 30 minutes in peak season, hourly off-season
- The five villages: Riomaggiore, Manarola, Corniglia, Vernazza, Monterosso (plus a hidden sixth)
- Food: 📍 Pizzeria Pulcinella (seafood pasta and pizza, about €13 a head) 📍 Trattoria La Scogliera (the only place open in winter in the second village; the seafood pasta is fantastic, €18.5)
- Where we stayed: Hotel Casa Dane (via Booking), NT$705 a night, close to the station, ⚠️ no lift
- Verdict: fewer places are open off-season, but with nobody around the photos are wonderful. If you're short on time, do villages **two, four and five** — Manarola, the second, is my favourite. The €2 tower in the fourth isn't worth climbing
- What it cost: accommodation 705, transport 560 return, food about 1,000

## Florence (day trip on 2024/1/22, my second visit)

- Getting there: about two hours by train from Pisa, and allow extra time for the ticket check at Florence station. The centre is walkable (40–50 minutes to Piazzale Michelangelo)
- Worth seeing: Mercato Centrale, Piazzale Michelangelo, Florence Cathedral, Piazza della Signoria, Uffizi, Accademia (the real David), Ponte Vecchio, Dante's house
- 💫 station → Mercato Centrale → Florence Cathedral → Piazza della Signoria → Ponte Vecchio → Piazzale Michelangelo
- Museum tickets are expensive (€2 for EU students — thanks, Brexit), so we made it a food tour instead
- Food: 📍 Da Nerbone (the €5 lampredotto roll in Mercato Centrale — a must, but the market is shut on Sundays) 📍 the grandmother's cart at the market entrance (lampredotto with tender spinach) 📍 Iginio Massari (cream bun €7) 📍 Caffè Gilli (cream bun, even creamier) 📍 La Strega Nocciola (gelato, two scoops €3.2) 📍 All'Antico Vinaio (billed as the world's best sandwich, €11 — overrated, but fun to share between a few people) 📍 Trattoria Zà Zà (upmarket: the T-bone is tender and on the rare side, the truffle pasta is rich, about €34 a head, book on their site)
- Verdict: climbing up to Piazzale Michelangelo at dusk for sunset over the whole city is stunning 🌅 Almost every toilet charges (€1 a go)
- What it cost: transport about 600, food about 1,700
