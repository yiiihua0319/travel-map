date: 2024/2/23–2024/2/26

## Getting around
- A transport card only pays off if you're staying a week or more. Buses take cards; a €2.5 single is valid for two hours
- With two or more people, Uber is the fastest option: a ten-minute ride is about €10–15, quick and cheap

## What to see
War museum, Popeye Village (skipped it, the ticket was too expensive), Triton Fountain, Upper Barrakka, Comino, Gozo

💫 What we actually did 💫
- 2/24 Sliema → Comino → Blue Lagoon → Gozo → Sliema → Valletta
- 2/25 the Three Cities → Valletta

## Food
- 📍 Cockney's Valletta Restaurant: the only place I'd recommend — BTS filmed Bon Voyage here! The seafood pasta is good; slightly pricey at about €20 a head
- Food in Malta is on the expensive side. To save money, order Bolt Food delivery (beef fried rice for €5!)

## Where I stayed
Boho Hostel: an old hostel that feels like your grandparents' house. The owner was lovely and so were the other guests, €29 for two nights (under €40 is cash only). A bit hard to find from the bus

## Things to know
- Mobile payments aren't well supported and cash is used a lot — withdraw plenty (about €10–30 a day)
- Get to Comino as early as you can: the Sliema ferry usually leaves around 10am and comes back at 4pm (it varies by season). Miss it and you'll have to go to Ċirkewwa for the boat (€15 return)
- Late February is about 10–20°C but the sea wind is fierce, with the odd shower

## What I thought
Comino is a must. The water is impossibly blue — sit on the shore with a pineapple drink, take photos, paddle about 🍍⛱️ The sand-coloured city walls feel wonderfully foreign. It's really a summer swimming destination (in February the sea is too cold). The heart of the Mediterranean 🏝️

## What it cost
- Accommodation: about NT$1,100 (two nights)
- Transport: about NT$2,100 (including ferry tickets and four taxis)
- Food: about NT$1,700
