Pairing Sweden with the Baltic states works really well — Stockholm to Tallinn is cheap and easy by either ferry or plane.

## Money
The Swedish krona to TWD was about 1:2.97 (April 2024). You barely need cash; I paid by card the whole time and never exchanged any krona at all.

## Getting around
- London Stansted → Stockholm Arlanda on a budget airline, £22.49 one way including baggage (ESN Card discount)
- Airport to the city, three options: the Arlanda Express, 20 minutes, 320 SEK / the Flygbussarna coach, 40 minutes, 149 SEK single or 209 SEK return (cheaper on their site) / bus plus metro, cheapest at about 42 SEK but with a change and 60 minutes
- In the city use the SL app: a single is 42 SEK (transfers within 75 minutes), 24-hour ticket 175 SEK, 72-hour ticket 350 SEK
- A day ticket covers buses, metro, light rail and even the commuter ferries! Get one and ride everything

## What we did
We used Stockholm mainly as a stopover and really only had one day. Museum tickets are expensive, so we made it a city walk.

Worth seeing: the Royal Palace, Nobel Prize Museum, Vasa Museum, Nordic Museum, Nationalmuseum, Viking Museum, City Hall, Kungsträdgården, Gamla Stan

💫 What we actually did (3/23) 💫
Nordic Museum (150 SEK, Swedish traditional culture) ▶️ IKEA ▶️ Gamla Stan ▶️ the Royal Palace ▶️ City Hall ▶️ Kungsträdgården ▶️ Nationalmuseum ▶️ a ferry at sunset

## Food
- 📍 Restaurang Kajsas Fisk: the fish soup everyone raves about
- IKEA: meatballs are the Swedish speciality (much like the ones in Taiwan, ha) and the cinnamon buns are NT$15 each — order one
- Supermarket cinnamon buns (vanilla or custard) make good gifts to take home; Lidl and Willy's are the cheapest

## What I thought
One day only, but I loved it: good weather, safe, clean and simple architecture. We wandered the city with no plan and no destination, then took a ferry at sunset — a quiet kind of happiness 😊 There are very few Asian tourists, so you can go round the museums in peace.
