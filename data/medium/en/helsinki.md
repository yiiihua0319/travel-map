date: 2024/1/25~2024/1/26 Helsinki, Finland
published: Feb 27, 2024

## Getting there
- London Stansted to Helsinki was only NT$790 one way (with an ESN card, 20kg of baggage is included)
- The centre is small and everything is walkable. The only transport you need is the airport train, about €4 (half an hour, frequent departures)

## What to see
There isn't a lot, so one day of walking is enough: Temppeliaukio Church, St. John's Church, Oodi Central Library, Helsinki Cathedral, City Hall (just walk past), Market Square, Uspenski Cathedral

💫 What we actually did 💫
Market Square → Uspenski Cathedral (free) → Manchurian Tiger 🥢 → Helsinki Cathedral → St. John's Church → the Moomin shop → Pho Viet 🥢 → the station

## Food
- 📍 Manchurian Tiger: €15 all-you-can-eat at lunch (à la carte in the evening; the guobaorou is excellent)
- 📍 Pho Viet: Vietnamese pho, and a bowl of it in a Nordic winter is pure joy

## Where I stayed
Hotel Arthur (via Booking): 8 minutes' walk from the station, comfortable, kettle in the room, about NT$1,267 a night. Great value, recommended

## What I thought
Not many sights, but I came away with a very good impression — beautiful buildings, that clean and simple Nordic look, and most toilets are free. For souvenirs I'd suggest the Finnish skincare brand **Lumene** (the sheet masks and moisturiser).

## What it cost
- Accommodation: NT$1,267
- Food: about NT$1,000 (+300 on supplies for the sleeper train)
- Souvenirs: a €1 postcard
