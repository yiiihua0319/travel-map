# #EuropeTravel — Rovaniemi, Finland 🇫🇮

source: https://medium.com/@yiiihua_0319/f667ac4f26d8
date: 2024/1/27~2024/1/28 Rovaniemi, Finland
published: Feb 27, 2024

My first time inside the Arctic Circle, at Santa Claus Village 🎅

## Getting there
- Helsinki → Rovaniemi: a 12-hour sleeper train (book on the VR site). Two departures, 19:00 and 23:00 — take the 19:00 if you want a full day there, since shops in the Nordics close at 8pm in winter
- Sleeper cabins are for two (you pay for two even booking alone, so travel in pairs). Upper-deck cabins have their own toilet, lower-deck cabins share. Lower deck is €89 a person, about NT$3,000 — an experience plus a night's accommodation, so good value. The shower is tiny and I hear there's no hot water
- Bus between town and Santa Claus Village is €7 return (prices vary by operator). On weekends there are barely any buses, so it's Uber

## What we did
- 1/27 Santa Claus Village (arrived 1pm) → husky sledding → All About Salmon → the northernmost McDonald's
- 1/28 Santa Claus Village → Santa's Salmon Place → Rovaniemi Airport

## Food
- Grilled salmon at Santa Claus Village is a flat €25 everywhere (NT$850 for one piece of fish…)
- 📍 All About Salmon: salmon soup €15, very creamy and good but not hot enough, comes with two flatbreads, about €21.25 a head
- 📍 Santa's Salmon Place: comes with bread and potato salad, and the salmon is juicier and tastier, €16.67 a head (all three of us agreed this one won)
- The northernmost McDonald's: you get a limited-edition postcard with your order, and the €4.95 Happy Meal is the cheap way to eat

## Where I stayed
Elice Apartment (via Booking): a six-person place with two bedrooms plus a sofa bed in the living room, lift in the building, about NT$1,387 per person per night, fully equipped kitchen so you can cook. The WiFi was patchy but the host was wonderful (I left a necklace behind and she messaged me the next day)

## Things to know
- To use the luggage lockers at Rovaniemi station you press the machine first and it **assigns you a locker** (largest size, 12 hours, €10.9)
- You can sign up for husky sledding on the spot but it sells out (the 2km was gone, so we only did 500m for €45)
- Photos with Santa are free; you only pay for a copy — €35 printed, €60 digital
- There are free toilets in the basement of Santa's building
- Finnish stamps are €2.5 each, and the post office at Santa Claus Village sells special editions. There's also a postbox that delivers your card a year later!
- If the weather is clear you can see the northern lights 🍀

## What it cost (excluding the sleeper train)
- Accommodation: NT$1,387
- Transport: about NT$500
- Food: about NT$1,500
- Souvenirs: about NT$1,000 (I went overboard on postcards and stamps)
