Came to Ireland for St. Patrick's Day ☘️ and added Northern Ireland while I was there.

## Getting around (things to know)
- ‼️ Ireland and the UK are different countries: Ireland is in the EU, so you go through immigration coming from Britain. Northern Ireland is the part that belongs to the UK
- Dublin Airport to the city: the express coach is €8–10; the ordinary buses (16 and 41) are €1.3–2.6, but they don't take credit cards — you need a Leap card (like an EasyCard) or exact cash (€2.6 a ride). In the centre you can walk everywhere (20–30 minutes at most)
- Dublin → Belfast: by train from Dublin Connolly, 2h15m. A flexible student return was €22.6 (adult returns are around €30). The bus takes about 2.5 hours
- In Belfast: the light rail is £1.7 a ride, buy at the machine by the stop, no tapping on or off

## What we did
- 3/17 the St. Patrick's Day parade in Dublin → the Spire → Dublin Castle → Trinity College → Grafton Street → The Temple Bar
- 3/18 train to Belfast → Titanic Belfast
- 3/19 day tour: Cushendun Caves → Giant's Causeway → The Dark Hedges → back to Dublin
- 3/20 wandering around Dublin

## Food
- Irish potato cake: mashed potato fried into a patty. Not really to my taste, ha
- 📍 The Rolling Donut: beautifully chewy dough, makes you happy
- Otherwise: yellow-sticker supermarket bargains and Britain's finest invention, the Meal Deal (main + snack + drink for about £3.5)

## Where I stayed
- 📍 Abbey Court hostel (very expensive over the festival): a bit dated but brilliantly located, free breakfast included
- In Belfast and on the last night we stayed in chain hotels; the last one was enormous and very comfortable

## What I thought
- The St. Patrick's Day parade wasn't what I expected — more like a procession of national advertisements. But the atmosphere in the city was wonderful: a Guinness and live music in The Temple Bar, and I got a free green hat
- One day is enough for central Dublin. Harry Potter fans should book Trinity College ahead (it's one of the classic locations). The gardens at Dublin Castle have cherry blossom in March 🌸
- **Titanic Belfast is superb.** Student ticket £19 plus £4 for the guide (student tickets are weekdays only). The flow of the exhibition, the sound design and the immersive shipyard section are all excellent — we spent three hours there
- The Northern Ireland coast day tour was £35 including transport and a guide. The coastline is beautiful, though it doesn't quite match Iceland
- Verdict: come to Ireland once you've got spare time 🤣 (I'd tick off the rest of Europe first)
