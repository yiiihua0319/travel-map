Came over from Munich at Christmas, and the city was full of Christmas 🎄 (three days, two nights)

## Before you go
- **Money**: Czechia has its own currency (the koruna) but you don't need to change much — cards and Apple Pay work almost everywhere. Christmas market stalls take cash and will usually accept euros too, at a slightly worse rate
- **Getting there**: Munich → Prague by train (no seat reservation needed with a Eurail Pass) or Flixbus. In the city a day ticket is 30 CZK, which is very cheap (buy it in the app or at a station; valid on buses, metro and trams). With a lot of luggage, take a Bolt — ten-odd minutes for about NT$200–300

## What to see
Lesser Town Square, Golden Lane, Prague Castle, the Dancing House, a Vltava river cruise (about £15 a person, booked through GetYourGuide; go during the day or in good weather), Charles Bridge at night, Old Town Square, the Astronomical Clock, Havelská Market, the Church of Our Lady before Týn, the Powder Tower

💫 What we actually did 💫
- 12/25 Vltava river cruise → Charles Bridge → Old Town Square → Church of Our Lady before Týn → Astronomical Clock → Powder Tower
- 12/26 Prague Castle → Lesser Town Square → the Dancing House → Havelská Market

## Food
- Chimney cake: an eastern European thing. Pricier in Prague than in Budapest, but they add ice cream
- 📍 Restaurace Mlejnice (our Christmas dinner, 20–30 minutes' wait): the onion soup was superb, the pork knuckle better than anything I had in Germany, the ribs quite strongly flavoured. Portions are huge and it's a lot of meat — unless you eat a lot, two people should share one. Order the home-made lemonade to cut through it. About NT$850 a head; highly, highly recommended
- 📍 Cafein (brunch, 10–20 minutes' wait): the taro latte is great fun, the croissants are enormous, and the avocado toast with chilli sauce — my boyfriend said it was the best brunch he'd had in Europe

## Where I stayed
a&o Prague Rhea: a chain hotel, a bit out from the centre but clean and tidy (taxis are cheap so it didn't matter). Just over NT$800 per person per night, which is cheap for the Christmas period

## What I thought
I loved Prague at Christmas — Old Town Square has a huge tree and the Christmas market. The sights are all close together and walkable if you stay central. One warning: nearly every toilet charges. I'd like to come back in summer.

## What it cost
- Accommodation: NT$1,623 (two nights)
- Food: NT$1,427
