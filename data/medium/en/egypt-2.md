# #Egypt10Days 🇪🇬 (part 2) — the day-by-day route, souvenirs and reflections

date: 2024/5/20~2024/5/29 Egypt
published: Aug 22, 2024

## Day by day

**5/20** Landed at SPX at 21:25 (from London Luton) → sprint through immigration for the visa and change money (the SIM card counter is past the exit) → hotel transfer, about 40 minutes and 700 EGP (with a group, inDrive is cheaper)

**5/21** Egyptian Museum (student ticket 230 EGP, with a Chinese-speaking guide) → the pyramids (student ticket 270 EGP, magnificent! May is brutally hot, bring an umbrella) → camel ride (included in the tour) → the Sphinx → dinner (an expensive place our guide took us to, with an unlisted 12% service charge — don't go) → Khan el-Khalili bazaar (about 40 minutes from Giza, roughly 130 EGP for a car of six; open until midnight)

**5/22** Set off for the Black and White Deserts (a 4-hour drive) → lunch at the owner's house in the oasis → switch to jeeps → Black Desert, hot and cold springs, date palm groves, salt lake (I'd cut this and spend the time sandboarding instead) → sandboarding down the dunes (enormous fun) → desert camping (no showers; cool at night and lots of mosquitoes; the desert barbecue was the best meal of the whole trip; falling asleep under the stars was wonderfully romantic, and there's even a signal out there)

**5/23** Up at 4 for sunrise → White Desert, Crystal Mountain → back to Cairo (4 hours) → shower at Africa Hostel (run-down but the owner is great) → 19:45 sleeper train (twin cabin $94.5 including two meals; app: Abela; get to the station 15–20 minutes early). Better than I expected — air conditioned and a clean toilet — though I got bitten by insects

**5/24** Arrived in Aswan just after 9 → three hours by car to Abu Simbel (student ticket 313.5 EGP; the 50 EGP return shuttle is worth it; the man inside who unlocks things so you can take photos wants a tip; read up on the history first or none of it makes sense; allow 1–1.5 hours) → lunch at a local place → our whole-house rental in Aswan (about NT$278 a person, and the view over Aswan from the roof is beautiful)

**5/25** Out at 6 → Elephantine Island (boat 10 EGP each way; really not worth it, it feels like a deserted island) → Philae Temple (card only! bring a physical card and your student card; I haggled the return boat down to 100 EGP a person, the going rate is 200–400; allow 1–1.5 hours) → a Nubian village (if you buy a camel sand bottle, check the sand is properly sealed — mine smudged on the way home) → 3–4 hours by car to Luxor → Enjoy Luxor → felucca on the Nile (wind-powered, with drinks and snacks on board, and a gorgeous sunset) → a Chinese restaurant (beef noodle soup at 180 EGP, cheaper than Taiwan)

**5/26** Hot air balloon (5am pickup, an hour in the air from 5:40 for the sunrise, under NT$1,000 — about ten times cheaper than Turkey; 32 people to a basket, so take an edge spot if you want photos) → Karnak Temple (230 EGP; my favourite of the whole trip, ten times more impressive than Philae; walk seven times round the scarab column and make a wish; allow 1.5 hours if you like photos; it's similar to Luxor Temple, so you could pick just one) → lunch (a partner restaurant, 355 EGP, overlooking the Nile) → a perfume shop (also a partner, but no hard sell) → Valley of the Kings (300 EGP for the first three tombs; the shuttle is actually 20 EGP return, so watch that the guide isn't overcharging) → a stone shop → a shisha lounge (the owner treated us; the fruit flavour with no smokiness was lovely)

**5/27** Left at 6 for Hurghada (4 hours) → Magic Hotel, four stars (about NT$700 a person, private beach) → ordered KFC on Talabat → the beach → the 300 EGP buffet, which I demolished → got pulled up to dance

**5/28** Red Sea day trip ($25 a person; dolphins, two snorkelling sessions of about an hour each, mask, life jacket and fins provided, bring your own towel; lunch and drinks included; GoPro photos and video $40, negotiable) → borrowed a hotel room to shower (free! we tipped $8) → 11pm night bus back to Cairo (inDrive to the GoBus station; tip 10 EGP each for loading and unloading)

**5/29** Reached Cairo at 6am → free shower at Africa Hostel and slept in the lobby (tipped 20 EGP) → Mall of Egypt (cards accepted nearly everywhere; Starbucks just over NT$70) → SPX airport, 23:00 flight to London

## Souvenirs

- Mostly from Khan el-Khalili; start haggling at 30% of the asking price
- Papyrus: hold it up to a light and an image appears, very cool, under NT$30 a sheet
- Camel sand bottles: about 100–150 EGP; check the sand is properly sealed
- White headscarves: I got three for 500 EGP

## Afterwards

After nearly a year in Europe and twenty-odd countries, most of what I'd seen was churches and castles. In Egypt every ruin, every site, even the ordinary street scenes were new to me — sleeping in the sand, camels and donkeys on the road, pyramids outside the hotel window, temples that make you feel very small. So many of my firsts belong to Egypt. I'd tell every exchange student to go (just not after April — it is unbelievably hot).
