Greece is in the euro zone. Bring plenty of cash — street stalls need it, and some shops will haggle if you pay cash. I flew London → Athens, and back to the UK from Santorini.

## Athens

### Getting around
- Airport to the city by metro is €9 one way (there are still trains at 11pm). Staying three days or more, get the three-day ticket at €20, which includes the airport both ways
- In the city a metro ride is €1.2 and valid for 90 minutes; a day ticket is €4.2

### What we did (4/14)
Asi Breakfast → the Acropolis and the Parthenon (student ticket €10, book a time slot on the official site; the climb is uneven so wear proper shoes, and the originals are in the museum next door) → Temple of Olympian Zeus → Street Souvlaki → Stani Yogurt → Monastiraki Square (a market, though the stalls all sell much the same thing)
It was already 28°C in mid-April. Food delivery in Athens comes right up to your hotel room, which is wonderfully convenient

### Food
- 📍 Asi Breakfast: run by a Chinese family, like a Taiwanese breakfast shop! Soy milk €0.8, ten xiaolongbao €2.5, beef noodle soup €6 — a real taste of home, highly recommended
- 📍 Street Souvlaki: traditional Greek grilled meat in pita, good, about €10 a meal
- 📍 Stani Yogurt: Greek yoghurt. The sheep's milk one is gamey, the cow's milk one is lovely
- 📍 Xia Wei: bubble tea; the brown sugar pearl milk tea tastes properly Taiwanese

### Where I stayed
Cosmos Hotel: dated but perfectly fine, about NT$800 per person per night, close to a metro station

## Santorini

### Getting around
- Athens → Santorini with Ryanair, €23 without baggage (7am flight)
- Renting a 125cc scooter on the island: there's a rental place next to the petrol station near the airport, €20 a day. You need an international driving permit plus your Taiwanese licence (the buses are not convenient)

### What we did
- 4/15: around Fira → ordered a fridge magnet at Black Velvet → Perissa Beach (empty in April, free parasols) → The Heart of Santorini (you have to walk down and the path looks wrong, but trust yourself — it's beautiful at the bottom, and a bit further on you can ring the bell) → dinner at The Dolphins → sunset at the lighthouse
- 4/16: slept in → lunch at Asia Club → rode to Oia (about 20 minutes from Fira) → the blue-domed church (queue for the photo), 3 Domes Viewpoint, Castle of Oia → gyros and the night view back in Fira. Every alley in Oia is photogenic; I want to go back 💓
- 4/17: one more dash to Oia in the morning (the wind nearly blew me off the scooter) → souvenirs in Fira → airport

### Souvenirs
- 📍 Bookstore and More…: sells stamps, and you can ask the owner about designs (the blue-domed church one is gorgeous). International stamps were €2 each (May 2024)
- 📍 Black Velvet: hand-painted magnets by an older gentleman, €8. Pick a design, then collect it half a day to a day later. Beautifully made — order early

### Food
- 📍 The Dolphins: a restaurant on the south coast. The seafood is good and slightly pricey, and the sunset is spectacular — a proposal sort of place
- 📍 Souvlaki Pita GYRO and More: pita with meat and chips, the budget option
- 📍 Asia Club Chinese Restaurant: good pork, add fried rice for €3.8, about €10–15 a head
- Greek supermarket yoghurt: the best berry yoghurt of my life

### Where I stayed
Nikolas Hotel: a holiday villa, just over NT$1,000 per person per night, lovely pool, free parking, 10 minutes by scooter from town. The owner has been to Taiwan and was incredibly warm. Highly recommended!

## Overall

By midday in April it's as hot as a Taiwanese summer, so wear sunscreen; evenings on the scooter are cool. April is the tail end of low season — few people and good weather (from May it gets crowded and expensive), so I'd suggest March or April. One of my three favourite places from the whole exchange year 💗
