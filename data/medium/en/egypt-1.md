# #Egypt10Days 🇪🇬 (part 1) — costs, preparation, package tours and accommodation

source: https://medium.com/@yiiihua_0319/bedbc61bd68a
date: 2024/5/20~2024/5/29 Egypt
published: Jun 7, 2024

A half-independent guide: see all of Egypt for under NT$20,000 excluding flights. Ten days and nine nights came to **NT$28,942** all in (under NT$25,000 if you strip out the optional extras and baggage).

## Where the money went

- Flights: London Luton direct to SPX (5 hours) £223.48, including £70 for 20kg of checked baggage. Friends flying from Paris, Barcelona or Budapest paid roughly NT$5,000–10,000. ⚠️ There is no WiFi at SPX airport, so look everything up beforehand
- Package tours, about NT$8,200 in total: Cairo + desert, two days and one night, $90 / Aswan and Luxor with a private driver, hot air balloon and felucca, $150 / a day out on the Red Sea, $25
- Accommodation: six nights in guesthouses for NT$1,679 (an average of NT$280 a night — the four-star place on the Red Sea at NT$700 pulled it up), plus about NT$1,800 on hostels used mainly for showers
- Food: NT$1,700 in total, roughly 200–300 EGP a meal. Corner shops are astonishingly cheap (crisps 10 EGP, 1.5L of water 10 EGP), and fresh juice and Starbucks are cheap too (a venti is under NT$80)
- Tickets: about NT$1,100 in total (May 2024 prices). A student card halves every single ticket! Most sites don't take cash but do take Apple Pay — bring a physical card to be safe
- Local transport: about NT$800 for short hops (inDrive is very cheap); long distance, NT$2,925 for the sleeper train and NT$292 for the night bus, so about NT$4,020 in total
- Tips: about NT$200 (200 EGP)
- Other: NT$40 on toilets, NT$420 on a WE SIM card
- Souvenirs: about NT$1,000

## Before you go

- **Visa**: visa on arrival, $25 (one counter at SPX takes a physical card). Sprint for it the moment you land or the queue will be endless; fill in the arrival card while you're queuing
- **Money**: bring US$300–350 in cash. Changing money at the airport is easiest (the rate was 1:46.59 at the time). Change only $100–120 at a time, because you can't change Egyptian pounds back into dollars. Plenty of places require a physical card
- **SIM card**: buy WE locally, 20GB for 600 EGP (haggle, and pay in Egyptian pounds for a better deal). It mostly worked even in the desert. A friend's Etisalat card was just over 300 EGP but dropped out occasionally
- **Student card**: essential, it more than halves entry tickets
- **Medicine**: ⚠️ bring as much stomach medicine as you can (seven of the eight of us got an upset stomach), plus insect bite cream and motion sickness tablets
- **Other**: instant noodles and snacks, travel-size toiletries (guesthouses almost never have conditioner), a towel and wet wipes
- **Delivery**: on the Red Sea you can use Talabat (cash or card)

## Getting around

- In cities use **inDrive**, where drivers bid for your fare and you pay cash. We squeezed six people into a saloon car at no extra charge
- Cairo → Aswan: sleeper train, a twin cabin at $90 plus $4.5 booking fee per person, about 13 hours with two meals included (there are biting insects on board, be warned). Book at https://abelatrains.com/ (they have an app)
- Hurghada → Cairo: night bus, roughly 300–500 EGP (I got mine for NT$219, 23:00→6:00; tip 10 EGP each for loading and unloading luggage). Book at https://go-bus.com/. Flying is about NT$2,000–3,000 for 1.5 hours — the night bus is very hard to sleep on

## Mindset and things to watch for

- Roll with it: sandstorms, flies and hotels losing water or power are all normal
- Haggling: shop around, open at 30% of the asking price, and never look like you want it
- Tips: the average monthly wage is 6,000–8,000 EGP, so 100 EGP is generous. Toilets are 5–10 EGP. If you're not happy, you don't have to tip
- ⚠️ Nothing in Egypt is free. If someone offers to take your photo or hands you something, they will ask for a tip
- For kids selling things: ignore them, or shout "one dollar" back at them and they'll run off

## Where we stayed

- Giza, **AliBaba Pyramids View**: NT$180 a night with breakfast, and you can see the pyramids from the roof — recommended. Airport transfer about $15 each way
- Cairo, **Africa Hostel**: about NT$100, used for a shower and a rest before the sleeper train. The power went out but the owner was lovely
- Aswan, **Private House with Garden and Terrace**: the whole house, about NT$300 per person per night. You can do laundry and cook and the view is lovely, though it's out of the way (fine if you have a driver). About fifteen minutes from Philae Temple
- Luxor, **Enjoy Luxor**: about NT$200 per person per night, close to where the felucca drops you off, and the owner will take you to a shisha place. Mildly recommended
- Hurghada, **Magic Hotel**: about NT$700 a night, private beach, pool and activities, and a buffet for just over NT$200. Highly recommended
