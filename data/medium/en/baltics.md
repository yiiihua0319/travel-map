# #EuropeTravel — The Baltic States 🇪🇪🇱🇻🇱🇹

source: https://medium.com/@yiiihua_0319/8f0ae3c94d84
date: 2024/3/24~2024/3/26 Tallinn & Riga & Vilnius
published: Apr 8, 2024

Easter break: flew from Sweden to Tallinn, then worked my way south into Poland.

## The route
- 3/24 Stockholm airport → Tallinn → the old town → Peppersack → Alexander Nevsky Cathedral → Kohtuotsa viewing platform
- 3/25 Tallinn 7:00🚌11:25 Riga → old town, Central Market, Museum of the Occupation, House of the Blackheads, Swedish Gate, Cat House, the Three Brothers → Riga 17:20🚌21:40 Vilnius
- 3/26 Trakai Island Castle → Halės Turgus market → the old town churches → Amber Museum → Užupis → Gediminas' Tower for sunset

## Estonia — Tallinn (suggested stay: half a day)
- Getting around: the centre is walkable. Airport to the city by Bolt is about €11 and 20 minutes; city centre to the bus station €4.4
- Food: pricier than I expected, close to western European levels. 📍 Peppersack, Estonian food: the duck leg and steak were unremarkable but the free bread was excellent, about €28.5 a head. For dinner I cooked in the guesthouse kitchen for €3.63 (cooking for yourself in Europe halves your food budget)
- Where I stayed: 📍 Tallinn City Apartment-Old Town (via Booking). Self check-in and hard to find, but cosy and with a kitchen, NT$895 per person per night
- Verdict: an ordinary small European city. All the churches charge, nothing is cheap, and apart from the lovely view from the platform I wouldn't especially recommend it

## Latvia — Riga (suggested stay: half a day to a day)
- Getting there: take **Luxexpress** from Tallinn (highly recommended — a big screen with films, excellent WiFi, free coffee, tea and hot chocolate, a clean toilet, roomy enough to sleep in, around €10 on average across the Baltics and Warsaw, and always on time). It's about fifteen minutes' walk from the bus station into town
- Food (all at the Central Market): filled buns €1.3 (like a Taiwanese doughnut twist with a fried meat filling) / dumplings €4.95 (they burst with juice just like Taiwanese ones) / a big box of strawberries €2
- Verdict: my favourite of the three! Cheap, tasty and easy on an Asian palate. We happened to arrive on a commemoration day and got into the Museum of the Occupation (Soviet history) free. The Swedish Gate and the Three Brothers are photo stops; St. John's Church is pretty from outside. Five hours in total

## Lithuania — Vilnius (suggested stay: 1–2 days)
- Getting there: Luxexpress from Riga. The centre is walkable and buses are €1. Trakai Island Castle is further out — 40 minutes by Bolt, about €25
- Food (Halės Turgus market): potato dumplings €5 (like crystal dumplings with meat, tasting of Taiwanese braised pork rice — loved them) / cabbage rolls €5 (the gravy was a bit odd) / cold beetroot soup €4 (cold and sour, absolutely not for me ❌) / traditional bread €1.89 (a croissant-shaped thing like a pepper bun, cheap and good). The bakery on the right as you walk in is fantastic, don't miss it
- Verdict: Trakai Island Castle is €5 with a student card, and it was my first red-brick castle — really striking. The interior of St. Teresa's Church is a beautiful pink and free (Vilnius has a lot of free churches). The Amber Museum is free too (amber is the regional speciality). Užupis is a tiny self-declared republic where you can get a visa stamp — the staff stamp it on pages that are multiples of 11, angel numbers — and it has its own constitution 😂 Gediminas' Tower is a 5–10 minute climb for the sunset

## On the Baltics as a whole
- My ranking: Latvia > Lithuania > Estonia (too cold and too expensive)
- The sights are all old towns, churches and architecture, so four days and three nights is plenty before it starts to blur
- Budget: I was very frugal and averaged NT$2,000 a day (cooking and cheap stays); NT$3,000–4,000 a day is a more realistic estimate
- Works well combined with Finland or Sweden (there are ferries), and there's a Luxexpress from Vilnius to Warsaw
